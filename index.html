OPEN LIBRARY

The Open Library follows a model you're probably already familiar with via image and video artists where the content is available for free, and those who wish to support the project can do so via Patreon.

Open Library titles are available to read for free with new stories every month. Stories are grouped by world, and a given world shares a consistent lore and mechancis between stories, even if those stories don't otherwise have overlaps. Click on a banner for futher details.

Elijah Dunham was born in our world, but one day he woke somewhere else entirely with no recollection as to how he got there, only a haunting feeling that he had done something terrible – a wrong only he can right. TITLE LISTING — WIZARD OF WEST CITY

Wizard of West City is largely episodic, though certain tales contain events that forever shape stories thereafter. The stories below are organized into periods punctuated by major stories. Non-major stories within a period can be read in any order.

"TECHNOCRACY"

A cure-all is the oldest scam, yet in a world of actualized magic, Eli cannot so easily dismiss the possibility. He sets out in pursuit of one such rumor and stumbles upon a nation of technology—of electricity and running water—and quickly finds himself in the middle of strange politics and an ongoing struggle between the unique sect of necromancers there and the Queen of Dragons who hunts them.

"TECHNOCRACY part 2"

Eli's quest to track down and consume a panacea has found him chained to a wall, waiting dissection from the necromancers who placed him there. His only chance for survival is the arrival of the Queen of Dragons and a Witch Hunter, having come here to kill his captors, but who would just as quickly kill him, should they discovere his secrets—a cure no less dangerous that the disease.

A long-standing tradition amongst necromancers was to read the Necronomicon—a tome of secrets written by Mors himself—and to take up the Death Mark as a sign of their bond to the Eldest God. Eli had no such need, as his magic is bound to his very soul and he could not fathom wearing a visible mark of one's blasphemy, let alone one that swears your fealty to a god. Yet something happened that caused him to reconsider.

TEMPORARILY DISCONTINUED
Terrantula was intended as a serial set in contemporary America in order to explore ideas that weren't commonly understood, yet were of common concern. However, as 2020 has continued on, it's become harder to understand how to write a contemporary story, especially one whose themes are now part of regular conversation between normal people. If the world ever calms down, I'll try to figure out how Terrantula fits back into where we settle. Until then, you can enjoy all both of the stories I had so far:

After a strange trip to a conference, science reporter Michael Collins returned to South Carolina to find he'd changed. Can he put these changes to use for good, or does the world need Michael Collins more than it needs the Terrantula?

TITLE LISTING — TERRANTULA

Terrantula is serial and meant to be read in chronological order.

Episode 1

Michael Collins is a small-time science reporter who's gone up to NYC to attend and report on a big science conference happening there.

When he wakes up the Sunday after, he can't remember the night before, but he's been given strange, new powers in exchange. Upon his safe return to South Carolina, it's up to him not only to figure out what his new powers can do, but what he should do with them.

Episode 2

MC's future as a science reporter continues to grow brighter with the addition of new segments and a new contact with strange new ideas, but his future as Terrantula looks rather more grim.

ABOUT + LINKS

I'm an author of fantasy, sci-fi, and horror with the "and" playing an important role as elements of each show up in every story, be it Sam of Grayhaven's scientific approach to magic, Erebus' encounters with eldritch powers in the far future, or just general unsettling atmospheres and descriptions. My education in computer science and math, as well as my hobby of gaming, also play into the way my stories are constructed and presented.

For updates on my work, follow me on Facebook. I also have a Twitter and Patreon page, which you can use to support my Open Library. I don't update these other social media pages as regularly due to a lack of following, but I'll be happy to change that, given the incentive.
